Searching Publication Finder

To search Publication Finder:

  1. Begin entering your keywords in the search box.

    As you type, keywords and searches matching your terms appear below the search box.

  2. Click search terms from the list to view results or use the drop-down menus above the search box to further refine your search.

  3. After making your selections, click the magnifying glass IMG on the left of the search box or press the Enter key on your keyboard.

  4. View your results below the search box.

  5. Click the Filter button to view the available filters for your results.

  6. Depending on how your institution's Publication Finder is configured, you may see options to filter your results by Peer Reviewed Journals, Subjects, Resources, and Databases in the column on the left.

  7. If a facet includes a search box, you can enter search terms in the box to find a specific term by which you would like to filter results.

Drop-Down Menu Definitions

The second searching drop-down menu includes the following options to help refine your result list.

  • Any

    • If "Any" is selected, it is the equivalent of searching with the Boolean OR between each of your terms.

  • Contains

    • If "Contains" is selected, it is the equivalent of searching with the Boolean AND between each of your terms.

  • Proximity

    • Finds your terms if they are within 5 words of one another.

  • Exact Phrase

    • If you select "Exact Phrase", This will result in returning list of titles using an exact phrase search.

      For example, if you search “Natural Neuroscience” using exact phrase, if there is title matching Natural Neuroscience, that title will be placed at top if list and remaining titles matching this phrase will follow (examples: Natural Neuroscience, Natural Neuroscience in climate change, Journal of Natural Neuroscience, Evolutionary Cognitive Natural Neuroscience (Cognitive neuroscience)

  • Exact Match

    • If you select "Exact Match", this will result in a list of titles using an exact match search.

      For Example, a search of “Natural Neuroscience” (quotes or not) will perform a “TERM” search and match the complete term in whatever search field is being searched. This will result in returning only one title matching the whole name Natural Neuroscience. If there is no matching title with the whole name, then no results are returned.

  • Begins With

    • If you select “Begins With”, this will result in a search using using truncation.

      For example, a search for Oil would return results for Oil* (Oils, Oiled, Oiling, etc.)
